CS 180 Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera


Submission by Daniel Jung. Thank you to Lindsey Wu for being the model for this project!

Selfie Comparison

Close-up (Distorted)

Stepped Back + Zoomed

When taking a close-up selfie, the camera is very close to the subject, which creates perspective distortion that makes facial features appear wider and more distorted. There are more relative differences in the angles between the features and the camera by taking a close-up selfie, while there are less relative differences by stepping back and zooming in. By stepping back and zooming in, we maintain the same subject size while reducing distortion, resulting in a more natural-looking portrait that better represents how the person actually appears.

Architectural Perspective

Zoomed In (Compressed)

Natural Perspective

The zoomed-in shot compresses the perspective, making buildings appear closer together and creating a flattened effect. The natural perspective shot shows the true depth and spatial relationships between architectural elements, demonstrating how zoom affects urban scene perception.

Dolly Zoom Effect

This shot was taken in the North Reading Room at Doe Library, UC Berkeley. The dolly zoom effect creates a disorienting visual where the subject appears to stay the same size while the background perspective dramatically changes, achieved by simultaneously moving the camera backward while zooming in.
